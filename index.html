Why this research

Open methods, where decisions about hunger relief get made.

Why open tools
Decisions made on data, not on intuition.

Decisions about hunger relief, food access, and pantry capacity often rely on instinct rather than measurement. The lab builds models food banks can audit and improve, with the methodology published alongside the result.

Who it's for
Operations staff, researchers, community programs.

Food bank operations staff making capacity and routing decisions. Public health researchers reviewing methodology. Community programs running their own pilots. Students learning food-systems analytics on real data.

What's open vs. held
Tools out, partners and findings held.

Tools and methodology are released openly. Pre-publication research, partner organization names, and unreleased findings stay private until the appropriate point of disclosure, in line with research agreements and community trust.
